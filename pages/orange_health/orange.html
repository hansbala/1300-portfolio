OrangeHealth

Pre-Design Thinking

COVID-19 pandemic and lockdown has made it extremely difficult for a lot of people to have access to essential medical services. India has been under a strict nationwide lockdown since March 22 that stopped all forms of public transportations. This created a severe health care insecurity for the people who lack a reliable medical facility in their region. Furthermore, people’s fear of contracting COVID-19 in hospitals discouraged them from making a visit.

A transition to telemedicine can not only help people access healthcare services more easily and safely, but also can prevent hospitals from crowding so that the people who need to be seen in person can be treated in a safer, and less crowded environment.

OrangeHealth is a startup that aims to solve healthcare insecurity in India by providing an online platform that connects doctors and patients for online health consulting, prescription, billing and so on.

With this in mind, we sought to build a comprehensive health care interface for patients that would include the functionality to register for and attend appointments, view health records, and otherwise contact the health care team. This same interface would have a similarly designed but intentionally distinct version for doctors, such that doctors can log in to view and attend their upcoming appointments, maintain their patient lists and health records, and manage a working calendar.

User Testing

Tasks

We divided our tasks into two scenarios with tasks. They are as follows:

  • Scenario 1 Imagine you’re a doctor in India (Dr. Tompkin), and you urgently need the patient number of Joseph Biden. Log in as a doctor, navigate to the patient portal through the dropdown, check Biden’s patient number, and log out.
  • Scenario 2 Imagine you’re a patient and you need to check when your next appointment with Dr. Tompkin, a doctor you connected with over the OrangeHealth app, is. Log in as a patient, check when the next meeting is scheduled, and log out.
  • Task 1: Without leaving the two homepages (after your log in as doctor or patient), in your own words, describe what do you think you can do on the two homepages? Be specific.
  • Task 2: Without leaving the two homepages (after you log in as doctor or patient), describe what you would select first. Do not select anything yet. If you would leave without clicking anywhere, please explain why.
  • Task 3: Without leaving the two homepages (after you log in as doctor or patient), what, if anything, helps this company stand out from its competitors? Explain your answer.

Analysis of Results
Video # Analysis
Video 1 User A analyzed the two homepages for the doctor and patient very well, but he neglected the use of the bottom navbar. User A’s comment about the homepages being very simple, straight to the point, and friendly for elderly users was expected. We designed the app to be user friendly to an audience of all ages.
Video 2 User B gave a very detailed and thorough review of the interface. Similar to User A, he commented that for a medicine related smartphone application, the application must be simple and understandable so that it can accommodate a wider audience. Our prototype was very user friendly and easy to understand. He commented that the navigation bar on the bottom may be confusing, but as a fully developed smartphone app, users would figure out what each button did in no time. It was also great to see that User B could identify exactly the functionalities of each homepage (patient and doctor) by simply glancing at it.
Video 3 User C was relatively quick in navigating to the messages and patient portal, which we believe was a good sign that the icons and the nav bar were pretty clear in what it was trying to do. He seemed a bit confused when he was doing the task of adding a new appointment to the calendar. (He kept going back and forth) User C commented that he particularly liked the “message” section and thought the usability of this app was pretty straightforward and clear. He also said that he liked how he didn’t have to leave the app to make an appointment, communicate with your practitioner, and even have a meeting with them. Lastly, he recommended making it a bit more aesthetically pleasing,but besides that he didn’t think our mockup needed much improvement.
